  CDC Pension Transition Simulator

  Live demo: https://alles-delenda-est.github.io/cdc-pension-simulator/

  What it does

  An interactive browser-based simulator modelling France's transition from pay-as-you-go (PAYG/répartition) pensions to
   full capitalisation, financed by CDC assets, HLM social housing sales, and employer contributions. The model
  implements 34 coupled equations over a 70-year horizon (2026–2096), tracking the legacy fund balance, sovereign debt
  trajectory, and individual capitalisation pot accumulation.

  Reform mechanism

  - Employee contributions (11.3% of gross wages) flow 100% to individual capitalisation accounts from Day 1
  - Employer contributions (16.5%) cover legacy pension deficits first; any surplus flows to capitalisation
  - CDC assets (€220bn) seed the legacy fund
  - HLM housing sales (5%/year of 5.3M units) generate capital gains for the legacy fund
  - Pension reductions (Equinoxe progressive curve) reduce legacy expenditure by ~26 Md€/year
  - Transition levy (30% of capitalisation flows from Year 15) accelerates debt repayment
  - Residual annual deficits are covered by sovereign borrowing (OAT issuance)

  Key features

  - 4 preset scenarios: Hypothèses de base, Original v5, Optimiste, Stress Test
  - ~25 adjustable parameters with tooltip explanations, including macro assumptions, return rates, contribution rates,
  HLM liquidation parameters, and pension reduction settings
  - Endogenous borrowing rate: sovereign risk premium rises with debt/GDP ratio (piecewise linear, calibrated to
  France/US/Italy precedent — no premium below 150% debt/GDP)
  - HLM volume-dependent price discount: accounts for market absorption constraints when selling large volumes of social
   housing
  - Equinoxe progressive pension reductions: continuous curve applied to pensions above €1,800/month, replacing the
  original step-function design
  - Monte Carlo simulation (100–1,000 runs): correlated stochastic shocks to returns, inflation, wages, and borrowing
  rates via Web Worker, producing fan charts with confidence intervals
  - 7 charts: legacy fund balance, pension split (PAYG vs capitalisation), sovereign debt trajectory, capitalisation
  pot, spread σ, contribution flows, cumulative NPV of legacy liabilities vs capitalisation payouts
  - Full data table with CSV export
  - URL parameter sharing: all settings encoded in the URL for reproducibility

  Technical stack

  React 19 + Vite + Recharts. Single-page application, no backend. The dist/ folder is fully self-contained and can be
  served from any static host or opened directly from the filesystem.

  Key assumptions and limitations

  - Cohort index is parametric, not actuarial — uses an 18-year half-life exponential decay with hard extinction at Year
   70, rather than INSEE mortality tables
  - Capitalisation payouts are stylised — ramp linearly from 0% to 100% over a 43-year career length, rather than
  modelling individual cohort vintages
  - No behavioural responses — retirement timing, labour supply, and precautionary savings effects are excluded
  - Wage bill grows uniformly — no cyclical or unemployment shocks
  - Single national HLM price — no Île-de-France vs province differentiation
